🔑 Key Moments of the Meeting

These are the most important moments. If you hear these kinds of things in a meeting, pay close attention.

🔴 Moment 1 — Report scanner errors
"If the gun doesn't allow you to do it how it's supposed to — go to the lead, go to me, come to Cisco. Don't just put it somewhere."

Why it's important: This is a direct instruction. If your scan gun doesn't work correctly, don't ignore it or try to fix it alone. Go to the lead or supervisor. An unreported error can damage the entire warehouse inventory.

Problem / Instruction
🔴 Moment 2 — Missing inventory items (blinds)
"I'm still missing two, but I found everything that was put on blind."

Why it's important: The supervisor is comparing the system against what's physically in the warehouse. If items in your area weren't scanned or placed incorrectly, it creates problems for the whole team.

Inventory problem
🟡 Moment 3 — Dock checklist not filled
"The dock checklist wasn't filled out. It's already Wednesday. Please, please, please make sure you're filling it out."

Why it's important: The dock checklist is mandatory every time that area is used. It was already Wednesday and hadn't been filled since Monday. When a supervisor repeats "please" three times, the issue is serious and has happened before.

Urgent task
🔵 Moment 4 — Patio rules & smoking area
"That is NOT an exit. Come in the front door, leave out the front door. Do NOT go out there and smoke."

Why it's important: Leaving through the patio doesn't register your entry/exit correctly — a safety and compliance issue. Smoking there is also prohibited. There is a specific area on the east side of the building.

Safety rule
🟡 Moment 5 — Tool change: tape gun → staple gun
"Think about putting staple guns at the back of the shipping desk. You have a staple gun — it's quicker."

Why it's important: Too many tape guns are being lost or worn out. The manager is suggesting a change for efficiency. This kind of announcement affects how you do your work that day.

Procedure change
🔴 Moment 6 — Broken air compressor
"The air compressor got a hole — we had to take it to the shop. We have to order a new one."

Why it's important: An expensive piece of equipment is out of service. This can affect certain tasks that day. Useful info to understand why something is not available normally.

Equipment problem

💬 Most Useful Phrases from This Meeting

These phrases came from the real recording. Learn them well — you will hear them many times at work.

"Really good job"
Muy buen trabajo

In context: The supervisor compliments the team at the start of the meeting. It signals that yesterday's work was good.

When you'll hear it: At the beginning of meetings, especially after a productive day.

💡 "Really good job yesterday — the numbers were great."
"Not as heavy to start out"
No tan ocupado al principio

In context: "Heavy" in warehouse = lots of work/orders. They're saying the day will start slow.

When you'll hear it: When the supervisor gives the day's workload forecast.

💡 "It's not as heavy today, so take your time on the floor."
"Go to the lead"
Ve con el líder / encargado

In context: The "lead" is the person in charge on the floor during your shift. When there's a problem you can't solve alone, you report to the lead.

When you'll hear it: Almost every time they talk about solving problems or errors.

💡 "If you're not sure, go to the lead — don't guess."
"Don't just put it somewhere"
No lo pongas en cualquier lugar

In context: Instruction not to place items without properly registering them. A warning about inventory disorder.

When you'll hear it: When they talk about inventory or merchandise handling.

💡 "If you find something on the floor, don't just put it somewhere — scan it first."
"Let somebody know"
Avísale a alguien

In context: One of the most important phrases in warehouse work. It means you should never stay silent if something is wrong. Always communicate problems.

When you'll hear it: In almost every meeting. One of the most common instructions.

💡 "If the scanner goes down, let somebody know right away."
"Please make sure you're filling it out"
Por favor asegúrate de llenarlo

In context: Urgent reminder to complete a form or checklist. When "please" is repeated several times, a problem already happened before.

When you'll hear it: When documents or forms are not being completed.

💡 "Please make sure you're filling out the timesheet before you leave."
"That is not an exit"
Eso no es una salida

In context: Safety rule. You must always use the official entry/exit so your scan in and out is recorded correctly.

When you'll hear it: During safety briefings or when someone used the wrong door.

💡 "The back door is not an exit — always use the front entrance."
"We'll see how it goes"
Ya veremos cómo sale

In context: Informal phrase meaning no definitive answer yet. The manager isn't promising something, but isn't ruling it out either.

When you'll hear it: When the supervisor can't confirm something yet — overtime, schedule changes, etc.

💡 "We might do overtime Friday — we'll see how it goes."
"Everybody got that?"
¿Todos entendieron?

In context: The supervisor checks that everyone heard and understood. This is the moment to ask if you didn't understand something.

When you'll hear it: After an important instruction, especially a safety one.

💡 "Come in through the front door only — everybody got that?"

📚 Important Vocabulary from This Meeting

Real words and expressions from the recording. Know these and you won't feel lost when you hear them.

Blind / BlindsArtículo sin ubicación asignada

In a warehouse, a "blind" is an item that is in the system but doesn't have a confirmed location. "Reconciling blinds" = verifying and assigning those items correctly.

🏭 "I found a blind in aisle 4 — I need to assign it a location."
ReconcilingReconciliar / Cuadrar el inventario

Comparing what the computer system says with what is physically in the warehouse. If the numbers don't match, there's a problem.

🏭 "We're reconciling the inventory at the end of the shift."
The gun / Scan gunLa pistola / El escáner

The barcode reader gun. "The gun gives you the red" = the scanner shows an error (red screen = something is wrong).

🏭 "My gun is giving me the red — I can't scan this item."
The redEl rojo / Error del escáner

When the scanner shows a red screen, there's an error. The item might be in the wrong place or there's a system issue. Never ignore it.

🏭 "If you get the red, stop and tell the lead right away."
The leadEl líder / El encargado del turno

The person responsible for the floor during your shift. Not the main manager, but the first person you report a problem to.

🏭 "Ask the lead if you're not sure where to put that pallet."
Dock checklistLista de verificación del muelle

A mandatory form that must be completed every time the dock area is used. Documents that everything was in order. Not optional.

🏭 "Don't forget to sign the dock checklist after receiving the shipment."
Scanning in / outEscanear al entrar y salir

The process of registering your entry and exit using a scanner at the correct door. Important for safety and keeping accurate records.

🏭 "Make sure you're scanning in through the front door every morning."
Dedicated smoking areaÁrea de fumadores designada

The only place where smoking is allowed. At this warehouse it's on the east side of the building. No smoking anywhere else.

🏭 "The dedicated smoking area is on the east side of the building."
Safety knifeCuchillo de seguridad

A box-cutting tool designed not to cut skin. Very common in warehouses. The meeting mentioned too many are being used/lost.

🏭 "Always use the safety knife to open boxes — never a regular blade."
Tape gunPistola de cinta adhesiva

Tool for applying tape to seal boxes before shipping. The meeting said too many are being used and lost each week.

🏭 "The tape gun is out of tape — grab a new roll from the supply room."
Staple gunGrapadora industrial

A tool that uses staples to secure boxes. The manager suggested using it instead of the tape gun because it's faster.

🏭 "Use the staple gun on the heavy boxes — it's faster and more secure."
Air compressorCompresor de aire

Machine that generates compressed air for pneumatic tools. If it breaks, certain tools don't work. This warehouse's compressor has a hole and is at the shop.

🏭 "The air compressor is broken, so we can't use the nail gun today."
Return transportÁrea / turno de devoluciones

The process or area dedicated to handling items coming back (returns). In the meeting, everyone was assigned there until 8:00.

🏭 "You're on return transport until 8 — after that, go to your regular station."
UppersPosiciones altas del estante

The top storage spaces on racks. Special equipment is needed to reach them.

🏭 "We need the reach truck to stock the uppers in aisle 7."
Schedule / On scheduleHorario / En horario

The planned timetable for tasks and workers. "Get back on schedule" = return to the planned routine or timing after a disruption.

🏭 "We need to get back on schedule — the morning was slower than usual."
Return transportÁrea / turno de devoluciones

The section or time period dedicated to processing returned items. In the meeting, everyone started there "till 8:00" (until 8 o'clock).

🏭 "You're on return transport until 8 — after that, head to your regular station."
SeatsUnidades / artículos (conteo)

In this warehouse context, "seats" refers to units or items counted in the inventory — not chairs. "700 seats in the back" = 700 units still stored in the back area.

🏭 "We still have over 500 seats in the back that need to be processed."
Shipping deskEscritorio / área de envíos

The workstation or area where outgoing packages are processed, sealed, and labeled before shipping. The manager mentioned putting staple guns there.

🏭 "Leave the completed boxes at the shipping desk for processing."
PatioPatio / área exterior

The outdoor area attached to the building. In this meeting, the manager made clear it is NOT an exit — workers cannot leave through there. It can be used to eat, but not to exit or smoke.

🏭 "You can eat on the patio during break, but always come back through the front door."
Full scheduleAgenda llena / muy ocupado

When someone says they have a "full schedule," it means their day is completely booked — no free time. The manager used this to explain why he couldn't bring breakfast.

🏭 "The manager has a full schedule today, so the meeting will be short."
DockMuelle de carga

The loading dock — the area where trucks deliver or pick up goods. Requires a checklist every time it is used. One of the most important areas in a warehouse.

🏭 "The truck is at the dock — make sure someone fills out the checklist."
InventoryInventario

The complete list and count of all items stored in the warehouse. Keeping inventory accurate is critical — every item must be scanned and in the right place.

🏭 "We need to keep the inventory accurate — always scan before you put something away."
Heavy (work context)Mucho trabajo / día ocupado

"Heavy" in warehouse means there is a lot of work — many orders, high volume. The opposite is "not as heavy" = slower day, less work. Has nothing to do with physical weight.

🏭 "It's going to be a heavy day — we have a big shipment coming in."

👂 What to listen for carefully

  • Numbers: goals, missing items, tool counts — how many of something.
  • Your work area, your name, or your shift being mentioned.
  • "Make sure", "please", "don't" — these are direct instructions for you.
  • Tool, process, or location changes that affect your job.
  • "Missing", "broken", "problem", "wrong" — something is not right.
  • Checklists or forms that you need to fill out.
  • Safety topics: exits, smoking, knives, compressors, scanning in/out.

⭐ The Most Important Things Mariannys Must Learn

This section is for you, Mariannys. Read it before every meeting to remember what matters most. 💪

1
🔴 If your scanner shows an error (the red), stop and tell someone

Don't try to fix it alone and don't ignore it. Go directly to the lead or supervisor. An unreported error can damage the entire team's inventory.

2
📋 Checklists and forms are mandatory

If there's a checklist to fill out (like the dock checklist), you must do it every time you use that equipment or area. It's not optional. When people forget, the supervisor notices.

3
🚪 Always use the correct door to enter and exit

The patio is not an exit. The front door is the only official entry/exit. This records your presence in the building — it's a safety matter.

4
🔁 When the supervisor repeats something, it's serious

In this meeting they said "please, please, please fill it out." When you hear an instruction repeated multiple times, it already caused a problem before. Pay extra attention to those moments.

5
🗣️ "Let somebody know" — the most important phrase in the warehouse

No matter what problem you have — broken scanner, item in the wrong place, not sure how to do something — always tell someone. Don't stay quiet. Communicating problems is part of your job.

6
🧠 You don't have to understand everything to participate

If you don't understand something in the meeting, don't worry. Listen for key words: area names, tools, numbers, and phrases with "make sure." With those, you'll catch the most important parts. With time, warehouse English becomes very familiar.

🌟 You can do this!

Warehouse English has its own vocabulary. Every meeting you listen to is real practice. Keep going — you're already doing exactly the right thing.
